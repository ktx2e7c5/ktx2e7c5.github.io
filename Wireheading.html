The Digital Arcana of 26B

2024-03-01

The digital arcana of 26B was legendary on the subcircles Sergio was familiar with frequenting, Sergio was explaining, moving quickly, wheeling around the bannisters up the fire exit. The basic gist was this: some whackhead got his share of The Spice and decided, high, to upload his brain info to a Cerebra system, appropo naught, and had performed the consummate ritual, attaching the magneto-encephalographs in the manner as diagrammed exhaustively by the manual handbook adjacently provisioned in preparation for appropriate mental scan. But in so doing, having fixated his attention, the effects of spice being to distract attention not otherwise fixed, inducing a sort of short-term dementia on their consumers such that the freshness of the moment invariably provides a countervening urgency to those preceding it—having affixed his attention on a synthetic video detailing (so rumoured) the slow growth of fungal complex through old Chinese villages, specifically those near the province of Yangshuo—having affixed himself therewith, he began the process of the brain scanning and, at the same instant, began to sober up.

The second thing about the Spin Doctor, Old Roll-the-Dice, was that it was fickle, and could turn you on and switch off at the click of a switchblade, it seemed like. So it's just as this machine is completing the scan, leavening alternative hemispheres, the entirety of which takes maybe 3 seconds entirely, that the brain undergoes a total electric and ecephalogic magnetic flip. You'll know (Sergio does his best Gene Kelly round the bannisters, practically vaulting the lower steps) that the idea of the Cerebra is that it creates into the electronic interface a space suit of sorts around the user's psyche that is precisely suitable to the ego in particular, and having been affixed, it will adapt to the changes of the ego as they understandably change. But if as this was happening the brain was to go through serious neurological shock, of the sort basically impossible in normal operating function, even on most drugs—it would be difficult to detail, precisely, what in actual effect would occur. But seemed safe to say it would not be 'entry-level', these after effects, or even inhabiting the ballpark reference class of cognitive-neurological systems that they were playing, Sergio and Montgomery themselves.

Sergio helped himself to the key for a fire door beneath a terracotta plant pot. Montgomery did not remember ever having seen this fire escape, let alone this plant pot. The carpet was a dirty duck-egg blue.

He hadn't been found for three days, Sergio continued, throwing open the fire escape door, until his landlord broke the door down looking for rent and found him there. Shit, sewage, the full Michael Caine. Non-proverbially plugged in.

Montgomery was struggling to hide that he was struggling to get his breath back. He paused on the top of the stairs as if to look down.

So an addict has a psychotic break and gets hooked on the Git, he managed. What else is up under the sun.

Not hooked, Sergio corrects with alacrity, off already, rounding corners. Because he's not there. He never logs in.

So he's braindead. Not kicking around.

So the landlord thinks when he calls the cops. But the cops aren't so sure. You see, the man's not out of the old race yet.

You've got a guy who hasn't had food and water in three days, you're saying, but he's not braindead?

Exactly this his eyes are twitching or his pulse is happening so they conclude this and what is more so they conclude the since man's not braindead, he must be being sustained.

Sergio is rounding corners, it seems to Montgomery, sweating, with something like the elegance of the regional skier.

He's being sustained is what he is. So they conclude it must be the Git. But not by the phenomena of the human world. See, when the policemen check the file log, they see that he never logged in. But that doesn't mean he's not online. Something is happening, he's making moves. Just not on any of Git's official communal servers.

So —

Sergio is at least two corners away now, his voice more like a disembodied nymph's. The walls are a lighter but similarly dirty shade of the same duck-egg blue.

So the detective concludes. Our aspirational amateur neurosurgeon goes up, hot and spicey and the like. Then he comes down. Misses the exit. Sideways shunted into some alternative cognitive-digital corridor. But he's still in the game, or hallucinating that he is, but the game believes he is.

And —

So they talk about moving him. They bring the chief of police in. They say, there's no way this guy is still alive, but his heart is pumping, and he's breathing. Show him the gamelogs. Show the water bill: he hasn't left the house or drunk a thing in three days. They deduce that the thing is keeping him alive. They cannot euthanise him. They elect to leave him there.

They're at another door. Sergio is rummaging for the key in his coat pocket. He is wearing a bright green jacket that seems to have pockets sewn onto the pockets. The halogen lamps make Montgomery slightly nauseous.

Landlord presumably furious, he attempts, weakly.

Sergio is rummaging in what seem to be several new pockets in the inside of his left jacket.

Landlord be damned about it. They bring scientists in. Neurologists, cardiovascular theorists. Try and understand what's pumping him. Best they can come up with is that he is legitimately operating in a simulation. His body and mind believe that they're getting everything they need, so there has been little degradation of the bodily functions. Needless to say they're going to need to study him.

Colonise the flat, you're saying. Occupy the residence.

So they — listen, what is it with you and property? Leave the landlord out of it. So they bring over squadrons. The box is held out in plastic. They hire an engineer and a heart surgeon to cut out the power cables, translate the box to the desk, power it on batteries. The function is still operating. He's still playing on real time. It's been five days. No food, no water, nothing. An extreme of human endurance.

Sergio places the emphasis of 'endurance' on the first syllable, like in the word 'amplitude'.

Here, hold this will you?

He hands Montgomery a large silver pestle, and continues to rummage more furiously. The pestle is shiny and reflective, except for a slightly powdered end. Montgomery tries not to think about it.

What's he playing? Can they tell?

Well, that's the thing. No idea. It's not Git, for starts. They try all sorts of digital interfaces. Some of them just start generating fractal mandelborat diagrams until they realise this is a fault with the interface. Others generate these weird pictures, like mirrors or eyes or something. They conclude that unless they've got a perfect neural net of his brain, they won't be able to understand what the particular matrix multiplications that the screen is reporting back to them. But fortunately — and this is where your friend comes in — they have neural interpretability siphons now. Technologies to map the mathematics of the brain as it's emerging at a, say, chemical level. Technologies to interface with him at the fundament.

So they try a few.

They try a few… Ah! Here it is. Knew it was in there. Here, I'll have that back. Where was — oh, most don't work. Then they train a thing called a reverse encoder—idea being that you're trying to generate real time data that the image is seeing. Think midreading but for a game. Well, this works. Sergio lowers his voice. He's somewhere alright.

Somewhere?

Somewhere. Reds, blues, that sort of thing. Yellows. Some kind of god maybe. Maybe some kinds of plants. Its difficult to say, though. So they hook him onto a drip. Sergio is quieter still.

Montgomery leans in. Yeah?

And he's still there today. Rumours say he got called up to A Block. Not sure what they're doing to him there.

He's still —

A.F.A.I.C.T.

So what's this?

Sergio leans in very close to Montgomery. His sweat smells like cut grass.

This, said Sergio, wheeling the key up to Montgomery's eyeline in a morendo flourish.

This, he proclaimed proudly, Is his room.
