Chrissie and Diana

2023-08

When Diana was low, she went to see Chrissie.

While Diana could not bring herself to say that she liked it, she had to concede Chrissie's room was a masterclass in some kind of aesthetic strategy: like feng shui and chaos theory mixed with ambient chic. There were musical instruments lain across the chairs, low lamps, pot plants with paintbrushes in the dishes, salt lamp, a Tibetan prayer flag, and something that looked like straw or sawdust in a corner. Encyclopaedia of Sound was playing some kind of classical contemporary beat (Nicolas Dick, One Void M'Appelle, Chrissie's favourite study music) low in the background. To Diana it sounded a bit like the Sopranos theme tune. There were papers scattered everywhere with Chrissie's trademark neat-seeming but utterly illegible print across them, all sorts of diagrams, splatters of paint, bright highlighters and gel-pens. There was a whiteboard with some sort of organisational map listing a few major institutions and the people working there, which might have seemed more world-colonisery if it hadn't also had a yellow post-it with a pot plant drawn on it saying 'remember to smile!' at the top. There were also three or four plates on the bedside table, each sporting different, half-finished food items, and a strong smell of cheap fruity incense.

Diana was barely in the door before Chrissie could hug her. She was wearing an Iron Maiden grey t-shirt (borrowed from Manny?) over a white long-sleeve t-shirt with green flecks of paint on the sleeves, wide jeans with rolled cuffs and red clogs. She wasn't wearing any make-up, but seemed to be emitting a glow.

Diana tried to perch on the part of the bed that wasn't buried under clothes or newspaper whilst Chrissie flew round 'tidying everything up', which largely consisted of her throwing things into the washing basket or the bin, seemingly arbitrarily.

"And then Rachel and Hernan went on a second date, but obviously she didn't know about Anastasia, which she only found out about because she joined the chem-bio anti-terrorist team Anastasia was working on, and heard her introducing Hernan to everyone as her boyfriend, and then she went off…"

"Mhm." Diana pushed some of the clothes on the bed in Chrissie's direction, being careful to use only the palm of her hand.

"Which is actually really shit, when you think about it, because Hernan is meant to be the risk modelling guy, he's meant to be the person that they come to when you want to model malicious actor behaviour in Central Asia or whatever, so when they used that drone in that attack last week…"

"The Turkmenistan one?"

"No, no, like, Pakistan or something. Not strictly Central Asia, more like the general region."

"Sure."

"…She's now got a conflict of interest deal set out, and has been transferred onto the nuclear/radiological weapons team, where of course she hasn't got any experience."

Diana gave up.

"How are you?" She said.

"Good! Busy."

Chrissie was putting the last of the paint pots back into a little hamper under the desk, which Diana had to admit was adorable. It had little Beatrix Potter rabbits on the side.

"You're… still doing anti-strike missile preparedness?"

"Eh. Just cybersecurity, mostly. Like data centre security and stuff. Less of the weapons assessment now that, you know, they're sort of already out there."

"Right."

"All pretty full on with all the data centre attacks in the Middle East and Brazil, at the moment. But lots to be done."

"Doing..?"

"Protecting compute centres, basically, to make it hard for people to start wars, incentivise against it… Something like that."

"And you're not worried about, like, superintelligence?"

"Oh, yeah, I'm pretty worried. But I think that's one of these 'dead on impact things'. If it is a problem, I don't think there's much we can, like, do."

She pulled off the Iron Maiden t-shirt and for the first time saw the blotch of paint on her sleeve.

"Oh fuck," she said. "That's annoying. This is vintage, actually. How cute is the back? Manny got it for me."

The back had an imitation of Van Gogh's Café with a Terrace in slightly peeling vinyl.

"Yeah, that is super nice," said Diana.

"Anyway! Sorry to talk shop and make you hold everything. Here, I'll take that." Diana hadn't even realised that she'd saved a small ceramic pot from the floor where it was begging to be stepped on. "How are you? How's it going?"

Diana rifled through her thoughts to try to find something appropriate to Caetano Veloso now playing on EoS.

"Okay. Busy. Actually, quite confused and overwhelmed, honestly."

"Oh yeah?" Chrissie put down a final cactus on a stack of zines and took a seat on the beanbag opposite. "How come?"

"Just… I mean… isn't this all a lot?"

She gestured around.

Chrissie looked blank for just enough time for Diana to realise she was alone.

"It does! No, totally." Chrissie said. "It's crazy. That there's not like… anyone else who's done US/China strategy with data… whatever. That we're running all of these streams, it feels like something…"

"It feels like fucking nuclear strategy. It feels like something someone who knows what they are doing should be doing."

"But you do know what you're doing, Diana. You're, like, one of the best qualified people here. If you don't know what you're doing we're all cooked. Don't undersell yourself!"

Diana leant back on the bed and put her hands behind her head.

"Aaaarrghhhhhh" she said.

"I know, right." Said Chrissie.

The sunlight was pushing through the blinds, striping the wall in cream barcode. It wasn't even 2pm.

"Would you like some tea?"

Diana grunted something affirmative. Chrissie threw a packet of Special K at her.

"You can munch that too, if you're hungry."

Diana lay on the bed putting handfuls of Special K into her mouth and watching the light scan the wall, listening to her breathing happen and trying not to think about the end of the world.

Encyclopaedia of Sound was saying something in the corner.

This song is Time Comes in Roses, by Bess Atwell.

Now for something different entirely, Diana thought, over and over, without really knowing why or where the reference came from. Now for something different entirely.

After a while Chrissie came back with the tea. One cup had a painting by Klimt on it, the other had Hulk Hogan's face with a smaller Hulk Hogan face where the mouth should have been. They looked funny from below, like two areola.

She looked at Diana lying there, staring at the ceiling.

"Dare I ask how things with Ben are going?"

Diana sat up, and Chrissie handed her the Klimt. Diana let out a long, low exhale.

"He's just… he's a dick, you know? Like he's lovely and we have such a nice time and then…"

"He doesn't text."

"No, he texts, but like.. he's totally out of it. Like he's just going through the motions."

"But then it starts up again?"

"Because then he'll reach out and just be like, what are you doing this evening! And we'll have a nice time!"

"Mm." Chrissie sipped her green tea. It smelt like flowers. "How is…?"

"Oh, I mean, that's fine. It's actually pretty wonderful."

Chrissie nodded sagely, holding Hulk's face in both hands.

"So what the fuck's the matter with him?"

Diana sat up and took a matcha biscuit from the plastic plate on Chrissie's bedside.

"I just think he's going through a lot," she said.

"Mm."

"But, like, of course he's going through stuff! I'm going through stuff! Everyone is! This whole fucking city is the first-world trauma department!"

Chrissie took a bite of biscuit.

"Sorry."

Diana stood up, and went over to Chrissie's bookshelf. On the wall, in between the half-finished wine bottles and plants and little flasks of gin, were a set of textbooks on forecasting, a few books on nuclear deterrence, an introduction to agent theory, and a Sally Rooney romance. She took out the romance. She nearly laughed.

"You have this? Remember when this felt like a big deal?"

"I mean, this is a big deal."

"No," said Diana. She sighed slightly. "It's not."

She sat back down on the bed and folded her hands on her knees.

"I'm going to get hurt by him, or I'm not. We're going to have fun together, and we're not. We'll break up, or we won't. Whatever happens to us, the problems in the world are going to be the same, and I don't have any solutions for them. I just… I just wish I was back in a world where I felt like relationships were everything."

"Listen to me." Chrissie stood up now and sat next to her. "Relationships are everything. Just because you're not some prodigy brain-dead shape-rotator doesn't mean you can't do great stuff. But you need to make relationships. And if that hurts, there's nothing in the world that can stop you from feeling that that sucks."

Diana took another handful of Special K from the box, and ate it, leaving small pieces of cornflake on the bedcovers.

"You can't sit in a box and forecast and figure it all out. Your brains don't mean you get away with this for free. But, you can have a really good time with the people you want to and make a difference to everything that's happening."

"I feel like this is… either an aspirational state of affairs you're describing, or more pep talk and less strategy than I need."

"See, now you're talking like my kind of psychopath again."

Chrissie clinked her cup to hers, so it looked like Hulk was getting in on the kiss.

"Also, guess what," Chrissie said.

"What?"

"I've started seeing Gavril."
